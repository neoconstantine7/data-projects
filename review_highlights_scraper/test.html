    This was one of the best dining experiences I've had in Cupertino, and definitely my new favorite Japanese spot in town!

    I must've driven by this place a billion times and never noticed it-- It's located in a plaza with a small supermarket.
    They walk you inside where you take off your shoes to enter the tatami floor. Their are a couple of private tatami rooms as well.

    I love the extensive menu of Japanese tapas cuisine! They have a beer, wine, and cocktail menu as well. Service was surprisingly fast for how busy they were!
    We had Salmon from their Carpaccio menu which were very fresh cuts and came with a light wasabi cucumber truffle oil.
    From their Salads, we got the Meyasai Salad which was a pile of pea sprouts atop mixed greens and a soy dressing--- it was great, light &fresh.
    The vegetable miso soup came with a generous portion of tofu and vegetables.
    In the deep fried menu, we chose the Nash Hasamiage, which was delicious and are slices of eggplant that sandwich a shrimp cake
    Of course we had to try one of their fusion pizza, which I was skeptical about at first, but actually ended up being one of the best pizzas I've ever had! The cheese: crust ratio on the Mentai Kinoko was prefect (lotsa cheese, very thin crust). They bake the pizza perfectly so it's lightly crunchy with gooey cheese. I couldn't really appreciate the taste of the roe & crab as much as the mushrooms and cheese though.

    Gochi has been on the center of my radar for about nine months. Since I moved up here in June, multiple people, I'm talking about dozens, have recommended I check out Gochi. I can't tell you how many times I've come close but plans fell through. Well, I wasn't going to let it stop me this time. Sandy recently got a raise at work so I wanted to celebrate. I made reservations last Tuesday for this past Friday at 8pm. The reservation process was painless & I got a table for two without a problem.

    KUMAMOTO & MIYAGI OYSTERS ~ 4 stars: Fresh, briny and sweet. They pour a little bit of ponzu in each shell. You can say it messes with the integrity of the oysters but it actually worked. Great presentation. yelp.com/user_local_phot…

    HAMACHI CARPACCIO ~ 4.5 stars: This was one of two dishes I was dying to try. Other Yelpers have raved about how amazing their hamachi is. Well, it didn't disappoint. Excellent flavor and amazing flavor. yelp.com/user_local_phot…

    AMBERJACK SASHIMI ~ 3 stars: This was kind of a letdown after the hamachi but it was still good. However, I wouldn't recommend this over anything else. I'd pass on this next time. yelp.com/user_local_phot…

    UNI & SCALLOP CARPACCIO ~ 2 stars: Not what we expected. The uni and scallops, surprisingly, were better when eaten separately. Together, it was a total bust. The scallops were lukewarm in the middle. It probably would have been better if they allowed it to cool down. Pass. yelp.com/user_local_phot…

    MENTAI KINOKO PIZZA ~ 2.5 stars: This was the other dish I was dying to try because fellow Yelpers have been j*zzing over it. I have to admit that the toppings were a tad weird - Crab, salmon roe, bacon and cheese. Well, I was really disappointed. To be honest, the disappointment I experienced with this pizza really brought down my overall dining experience. Although it wasn't bad, it just felt like a pizza that was thrown together by a drunk college kid with access to crab meat. yelp.com/user_local_phot…

    BAMBOO & SEAWEED IN DASHI BROTH ~ 2 stars: Just don't ever get this. I don't even want to elaborate. yelp.com/user_local_phot…

    IKO GOBO KAKIAGE ~ 4.5 stars: OH. MY. GOD. This might have been my favorite dish of the night. Try to imagine shoestring fries, tempura and calamari producing a three-way love child. That's exactly what this is. Splash it with some lemon and dip it in the ponzu and you get a crunchy, moist and citrus mouth orgasm. yelp.com/user_local_phot…

    BRAISED TONGUE w/ POTATO PUREE ~ 3 stars: It looks like pot roast. It tastes like pot roast. In my book that's not a bad thing. (The picture didn't come out well so I didn't post it)

    GARLIC GREEN SPINACH SALAD ~ 3 stars: A refreshing salad with daikon, fried garlic and a soy dressing. Nothing special about this but it was good. yelp.com/user_local_phot…

    EXTRA FATTY BLUEFIN TUNA ~ 4.5 stars: I will say without any hesitation that this was the best sashimi I've had in The Bay Area. The fatty bluefin melted in my mouth after the sweetness of the fish hit my palette. Absolutely amazing. It left us speechless. yelp.com/user_local_phot…

    GREEN TEA CREME BRULEE ~ 4 stars: I don't like desert, however, this was great. It wasn't that sweet and it was a perfect way to end our meal. yelp.com/user_local_phot…

    Gochi didn't disappoint me but it might underwhelm some people. It's one of those restaurants that can go either way - you'll either love it or hate it. I have to admit that while I was dining there, I kept leaning back and forth on whether or not I liked it. They had me walking a really fine line, but at the end I enjoyed everything. We ordered 11 dishes and it came out to $143.36 pre-tax. It's pricey but you have to realize it's not the kind of place you'll dine at all the time... Or, at least, you shouldn't. It's the kind of joint you go to when you feel like splurging.

    I suggest not going off of what other Yelpers are recommending but do take their reviews into consideration. Go into Gochi blind and feel out the menu. Check out what other people around you are eating and ask your server questions. Their menu is pretty extensive so it might be a bit overwhelming but trust your gut. We did and although some dishes disappointed, we also discovered some bomb ass stuff (i.e. IKO GOBO KAKIAGE).

    Gochi Fusion Tapas is a fun place to grab lunch or dinner. It has a traditional Japanese dining ambiance and an extensive menu. The small plates are perfect for sharing on a date or with a group of family or friends. TIP: make reservations in advance, especially if you are dining with a larger party! It gets packed during peak times. You can request seating at a table or "traditional" Japanese style on the floor.  

    For lunch, the Tori Nanban Don is a steal at $11.50. The deep fried chicken and eggplant pair perfectly with the soy-garlic vinaigrette. I am drooling just thinking about this combo right now. Go for the full donburi + salad + miso soup set instead of the small donburi + udon set. I guarantee you will devour it! The menu also has a large selection of donburi and udon and bento to peruse.  

    Dinner: fish enthusiasts will love their carpaccios -- my favorites are the sake and hamachi. Meat lovers will go crazy for the buta kakuni. Nothing beats tender pork with divine traditional japanese flavors. The fusion cheese pizzas are fun too! For dessert, the green tea creme brulee was smooth and creamy, though the portion was tiny!  

    And, what Japanese meal is complete without black cod! The Gindara Munieru does melt in your mouth but the minuscule portion is definitely not worth $16.50. I recommend skipping this and ordering something else to get a better return on investment. My Mom insists on ordering the Mabo tofu Meshi clay pot every time we come here. The spicy pork, tofu, and crispy rice combo is tasty, but I'd rather order different specialities on the menu that I can't easily find at other restaurants. If you do order a clay pot, go for the unagi instead. Tender Unagi + savory unagi sauce + crispy rice is a true winning combo. A couple times our clay pots have come out burnt, so be sure to remind your server that you don't want the rice overcooked.

    The black cod was easily some of the best food I've had in the area in a long time.  The skin was crunchy and flavorful-- I could eat that skin for dinner just by itself.  The fish itself was cooked perfectly, with it easily flaking as I poked my chopsticks in.  It soaked up the sauce beautifully.  And who can forget the asparagus?  Easily not noticed as a garnish, but it was crunchy, with just subtle asparagus flavor, and it soaked up the sauce too.  If we had asparagus like that growing up, I'd totally be an asparagus fan.  

    - Value (5/5)
    With the hamachi carpaccio, you get 8 pieces, which even as a sashimi order would be great value-- then they treat it with a sauce, and you know you've got a great deal.  The frisee salad was the big downer since they didn't use that many mushrooms.  Essentially, we paid $13 for a bunch of frisee (my poor choice).  The gyutan shioyaki might've also been slightly overpriced, though there were 6-8 sizable pieces.  The best value of the night was the gindaru munieru.  Two good pieces of superb black cod, and I'd pay far more than $16.50.  In spite of some lost value in the salad and the beef tongue, the meal as a whole was certainly exceptional value.  

    Final note: I'd love to go back to Gochi to sample more dishes.  Given the right combination of dishes (like the black cod), this easily turns into a 5 star rating.  It's just that given some of the misses with the salad, and the less than memorable nature of the beef tongue, I find it difficult to elevate it to the coveted 5 stars just yet.  Stay tuned for an update...
